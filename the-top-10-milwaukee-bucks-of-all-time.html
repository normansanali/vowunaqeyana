The Top 10 Milwaukee Bucks of All-Time

July 12, 2024
blog

The Milwaukee Bucks, founded in 1968, have had their share of legendary players who have left an indelible mark on the franchise and the NBA as a whole.

From dominant big men to electrifying guards, the Bucks' history is filled with iconic figures who have shaped the team's identity.

1. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, formerly known as Lew Alcindor, tops the list as the most dominant player in Bucks history. In his six seasons with the team, he averaged an astounding 30.4 points, 15.3 rebounds, and 3.4 assists per game.

Abdul-Jabbar led the Bucks to their first and only NBA championship in 1971 and earned three MVP awards during his tenure in Milwaukee.

2. Oscar Robertson

The acquisition of Oscar Robertson in 1970 formed one of the most formidable duos in NBA history alongside Kareem Abdul-Jabbar.

Robertson's impact on the Bucks was immediate, as he contributed 19.4 points, 8.2 rebounds, and 5.7 assists per game during his four seasons with the team.

His leadership and playmaking abilities were instrumental in the Bucks' championship run in 1971.

3. Sidney Moncrief

Sidney Moncrief, a two-time NBA Defensive Player of the Year, was the heart and soul of the Bucks in the 1980s.

Known for his tenacious defense and versatile scoring, Moncrief played 10 seasons in Milwaukee, earning five All-Star selections and setting the standard for excellence on both ends of the floor.

4. Ray Allen

Ray Allen, a prolific shooter and scorer, made a significant impact during his tenure with the Bucks.

His smooth shooting stroke and ability to create his own shot made him a fan favorite, and he was a key player for the Bucks during his six seasons with the team. 

5. Giannis Antetokounmpo

A modern-day superstar, Giannis Antetokounmpo has already etched his name in Bucks history.

The "Greek Freak" has captured multiple MVP awards, led the Bucks to an NBA championship in 2021, and continues to mesmerize fans with his incredible athleticism and skill set. His impact on both ends of the floor cements his legacy as one of the top players in franchise history.

6. Bob Dandridge

Bob Dandridge, a key contributor to the Bucks' championship run in 1971, was known for his scoring ability and defensive prowess. Dandridge's versatility and clutch performances in the playoffs solidified his place among the all-time greats.

7. Glenn Robinson

Glenn Robinson, affectionately known as "Big Dog," was a scoring machine for the Bucks during the 1990s. His ability to fill up the stat sheet and carry the offensive load made him a cornerstone of the team during his eight seasons in Milwaukee.

8. Jon McGlocklin

Jon McGlocklin, a sharpshooting guard, spent the majority of his career with the Bucks and was instrumental in the team's early success. 

9. Marques Johnson

Marques Johnson, a versatile forward, showcased his scoring and playmaking abilities during his seven seasons with the Bucks. Johnson's impact on the offensive end and his ability to elevate the play of his teammates make him a beloved figure in Bucks lore.

10. Michael Redd

Michael Redd, known for his lethal scoring prowess, left an indelible mark on the Bucks during his 11 seasons with the team. His ability to light up the scoreboard and carry the offensive load solidified his status as one of the top scorers in franchise history.

 

The Milwaukee Bucks have been home to a plethora of iconic players who have shaped the franchise's history and left an enduring legacy.

From championship-winning superstars to scoring machines and defensive stalwarts, the Bucks' all-time greats have made an indelible impact on the team and the NBA as a whole.

As the franchise continues to evolve, these legendary figures will forever hold a special place in the hearts of Bucks fans worldwide.

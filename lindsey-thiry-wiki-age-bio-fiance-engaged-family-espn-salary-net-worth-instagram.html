Lindsey Thiry Biography – Lindsey Thiry Wiki

Lindsey Thiry is an American sports journalist from Seattle Washington. She is the Los Angeles Rams team reporter for ESPN’s NFL Nation. She joined the ESPN team in May 2018. Thiry covers the Los Angeles Rams for ESPN.com, she also has other multimedia roles which include appearances on other ESPN platforms, including NFL Live, SportsCenter, and ESPN Radio.

Prior to joining ESPN, Thiry served as a multimedia sports reporter for four years at the Los Angeles Times. While there she covered the Los Angeles Rams, Los Angeles Dodgers, Lakers, the University of Southern California, and University of California, Los Angeles. At Los Angeles Times, Thiry won an Associated Press Sports Editors’ award for a short video in 2016. Before she joined the Los Angeles Times, Thiry covered college football and recruiting for Scout.com, and appeared on Fox Sports 1, Fox Sports West, and Spectrum SportsNet.

She was born in Seattle Washington. She attended Seattle preparatory school where she was active in volleyball. Thiry attended the University of Washington, where she graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Communications. While there she was a Member of the 2005 NCAA National Championship Women’s Volleyball team. She interned with the Seattle Mainers in 2008. In her free time, she enjoys reading books.

Lavender Darcangelo: Is She Deaf? Health And Disability in 2023

Yes, Lavender Darcangelo, a gifted vocalist and competitor on AGT season 18, is blind.

Moreover, Darcangelo has autism as well. Although the 27-year-old from Fitchburg, Massachusetts, didn’t start speaking until she was four years old, her musical adventure started at the young age of three.

The gifted singer moved in with Wil Darcangelo, the supervisor of the after-school program, and his girlfriend when she was fifteen.

When Lavender was 22 years old seven years later, this loving couple officially adopted her.

When Lavender’s captivating rendition of “Part of Your World” from Disney’s The Little Mermaid went viral in 2019, she garnered recognition from the general public.

Her performance in the reality singing competition has been amazing, demonstrating not only her amazing vocal ability but also her enthusiasm and dedication.

Since the outset, she has been upfront about her impairment. It seems that the America’s Got Talent finalist used music to express herself and overcome obstacles.

She serves as an inspiration to those who experience disability or feel differently. The talented soprano shared her experience with bravery and candor.

Golden Buzzer Lavender Darcangelo was chosen

Heidi Klum, a model and judge, was among the audience members who found her performance moving enough to push the recognizable Golden Buzzer.

The singer’s path reached a turning point when she received this support, which sent her directly into live performances.

Lavender’s skill and perseverance so impressed Heidi Klum that he expressed how glad his heart was.

“I’m going to hold your hand, and we’re going to go all the way to the finish line,” he said as motivation to the singer.

Lavender’s current status as a finalist in the competition is evidence of her extraordinary talent.

Coming Soon: Lavender Darcangelo’s Debut Album

Lavender Darcangelo is an incredible AGT competitor who is also a talented young artist. According to her website, she is now working on “Mosaic,” her first solo album.

It is clear that Darcangelo is committed to creating music that uplifts and speaks to her community.

She conducted a free online performance in celebration of Disability Awareness Month in October 2020.

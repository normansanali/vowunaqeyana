Christopher Michael Settlemirewas born on the 9th May 1985, in Warren, Ohio USA, of mixed British, German, Polish and Czech ancestry, and better known as Chris Zylka is an actor, probably best recognized for starring in the role of Flash Thompson in the film “The Amazing Spider-Man”, and playing Tom Garvey in the TV series “The Leftovers”. His career has been active since 2008.

Chris Zylka spent his childhood in his hometown, where he was raised by his parents. Upon matriculation from Howland High School in 2003, he enrolled in the University of Toledo, but there is no record of him graduating.

Prior to his acting career, Chris worked as a fashion model, who collaborated with such brands as Diesel Jeans and Calvin Klein. He was also on the cover of several magazines, including VMan, Bello, Homme Essential and Glamoholic, among others, all of which marked the establishment of his net worth.

Speaking about Chris’ career as an actor, it began in 2008, when he guest-starred in the TV series “Everybody Hates Chris”, which was followed by another guest-starring appearance in the TV series “90210”. His breakthrough came in 2009, when he featured in the role of Brigg in the TV film “My Super Psycho Sweet 16”, which he later reprised in its spin-off “My Super Psycho Sweet 16: Part 2” (2010). During the same year, he was chosen to play Joey Donner in the TV series entitled “10 Things I Hate About You” (2009-2010), after which he portrayed Thor in the film “Kaboom” (2010), directed by Gregg Araki. All of these appearances added a considerable amount to his net worth.

In 2011, Chris was selected to portray Jake Armstrong in the TV series “The Secret Circle”, which lasted for a season. Parallel with filming it, he was also cast as Blake in the 2011 film “Shark Night 3D”, and Kyle in the film “Piranha 3DD” (2012). His next major role came when he featured as Flash Thompson in Marc Webb’s film “The Amazing Spider-Man” (2012), starring along with Andrew Garfield and Emma Stone, which was followed by his portrayal of Mr. Hottie in the film “The People I’ve Slept With” in the same year. All of these roles increased his net worth by a large margin.

To speak further about his career, Chris was chosen to portray Tom Garvey in the TV series “The Leftovers” from 2014 to 2017, contributing steadily to his net worth. He also starred in the role of Kermit in Hank Bedford’s film “Dixieland” (2015), and featured as Jeremiah in the 2016 film “Welcome To Willits”. Most recently, he landed the role of Chuck Harris in the film “Novitiate”, and it has been announced that he will appear as Will Jefford Jr. in the film “The Death And Life Of John F. Donovan” in 2018. His net worth is certainly rising.

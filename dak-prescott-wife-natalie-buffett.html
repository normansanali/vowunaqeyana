The Rise of Dak Prescott: A Star Quarterback Born and Raised

Nathaniel and Peggy Prescott’s youngest son, Dak, was born on July 29, 1993. Despite the challenges she faced, Peggy instilled a love of football in her son at a young age, setting him on the path to greatness.

Haughton High School: A Star in the Making

Dak attended Haughton High School, where he played for the school’s football team. He quickly established himself as a standout player, leading the team to victory in the 2010 District 1-AAAA Championship. His impressive performance on the field earned him recognition and paved the way for his future success.

Mississippi State University: A Rising Star

After graduating from high school, Dak continued his football career at Mississippi State University. He spent the first two years as Tyler Russell’s backup, but was able to start for the team when Russell suffered a concussion in 2013. Dak’s performance was outstanding, and he was named the Most Valuable Player at the 2013 Liberty Bowl, where the Bulldogs beat the Rice Owls 44-7. He was also put on the 2013 SEC Fall Academic Honor Roll after the season.

Full-Time Starter: Breaking Records and Winning Awards

In 2014, Dak became a full-time starter for Mississippi State University and his play continued to improve. He led the team to the Orange Bowl, broke several records, and won the Conerly Trophy for his impressive performance. He was also a finalist for numerous prestigious awards, including the Manning Award, the Johnny Unitas Golden Arm Award, the Davey O’Brien Award, and the Maxwell Award.

A National Star: The 2015 Season

In 2015, Dak’s last year of college football, he continued to shine. He was named the AutoNation National Offensive Player of the Week by the Football Writers Association of America, the Davey O’Brien Award National Quarterback of the Week, a Manning Award Star of the Week by the Allstate Sugar Bowl, and the SEC offensive player of the week for the fourth time after passing for 348 yards and three touchdowns in the Bulldogs’ win over Kentucky.

Morgan Fogarty Biography

Morgan Fogarty is a news reporter/anchor who anchors the Emmy-award-winning WCCB News @ Ten. She was born on 23rd May 1982 in the Pennsylvania United States.

Morgan also reports on a regular basis, covering topics like local crime, politics, and neighborhood issues, Morgan hosts and moderates WCCB News Edge at 10:30, alongside regular panelists Matt Harris, Ashley Anderson, and Tremaine “QCB” Sloane. The Edge is Charlotte’s premiere news magazine show and features an in-depth conversation about news, sports, politics, and pop culture.

Morgan graduated from Penn State University with a B.A. in Broadcast Journalism with a minor in Theatre. Morgan travels for special assignments as well as breaking news, to places like Washington DC, to cover the Lying in Honor of the late Rev. Billy Graham, to Charlottesville to cover the deadly white nationalist rally, to Boston to cover new active shooter alert technology, as well as to San Francisco, to cover the Carolina Panthers 2016 Super Bowl run.

Morgan’s series, “The Get with Morgan Fogarty,” features in-depth interviews with celebrities and newsmakers, including Carolina Panthers Quarterback Cam Newton, Elevation Church Pastor Steven Furtick, and Bachelorette Emily Maynard, NASCAR’s Dale Earnhardt, Jr., and North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper.

She has established herself as an animal welfare advocate and Morgan has earned the trust of animal lovers in our community, who turn to her when they need to raise awareness about animal issues.

She has won numerous awards throughout her career including several from the Radio Television Digital News Association of the Carolinas. In 2010, Morgan earned 1st Place for her series called “Pit Bull Problems.” The same year, she earned 2nd place in the North Carolina TV Reporter of the Year division. In the year 2009, Morgan won 1st place in the health/medicine division for a story about the so-called “Chicken Pox Parties.”

Over the years, Charlotte area viewers have voted her “favorite anchor,” “best TV anchor” as well as more in local publications. In 2012, Morgan was named one of Charlotte’s “Top 30 Under 30 Future Leaders.” In the same year, Morgan won the National Athletic Trainers’ Association Excellence in Sports Medicine Reporting Award for her report on concussions in Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools.

In 2015, she was named one of the 50 Most Influential Women by The Mecklenburg Times. In 2017, Morgan was awarded the Community Service Media Award by Keystone Substance Abuse Services for her two part-feature “Gone Too Soon: Heroin Deaths in Charlotte.”

Even though Morgan was born a Yankee, she has now lived in Charlotte longer than she’s lived anywhere else. Morgan considers Charlotte “home” and can’t imagine leaving (she tried once, in 2013, went to New York City, and quickly returned!). Morgan, her husband along their two children share their home with three dogs: two French bulldogs, Winston and Etta (a rescue), and a Doberman, Rachel (also a rescue).

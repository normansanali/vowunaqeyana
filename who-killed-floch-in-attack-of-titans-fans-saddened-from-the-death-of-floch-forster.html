Floch Foster was one of the most well-written characters in AOT, and his character development could very well compete with Eren and Reiner. Who killed Floch Foster? Learn more below.

Floch Foster is a well-written character, and if judged by modern standards and morals, he would be an absolutely horrible person. However, his growth as a person has been such a well-put story that Floch is one of the most beloved characters in the series.

Floch died for his beliefs, and honestly, his death was one of the most exhilarating and awe-inspiring moments.

Who Killed Floch In AOT? Death Cause Of Floch Forster

Floch Foster was shot by Gaby. He was trying to sink the boat with a Thunder Spear, but the shot made him plummet down to the ocean, badly injured.

In the anime, Floch’s death is action-packed close-up shots, fluid camera motion, and rapid change of pace. It all highlights the tension and the adrenaline of the fight and the climax with Floch’s death.

However, the death of Floch Foster focuses more on his character development. In the manga, Floch manages to hook his maneuvering gear to the boat, and he follows the group across the ocean to Odiha.

At his last breath, Floch drags himself onto the hangar. In front of Hange and Jean, Floch begs, not for his life, but his home.

Floch was a self-proclaimed coward, but his growth as a person and his belief of wanting to save his home couldn’t have been more real.

Floch Foster On Wikipedia

Floch Foster has his history written in the official Attack On Titan Wikipedia. He was one the most well-written character in the series, ranking only after Eren and Reiner.

Floch was a former recruit of Survey Corps, and he was the only survivor of the battle except for Hange and Squad Levi.

He was a prominent Yeagrists, and he was the de-facto leader of the faction. He was a self-proclaimed coward, but he wasn’t afraid of death. He was scared of his death not being meaningful.

He believed Eren was the only one who could save the New Eldian Empire and a necessary evil.

Floch Foster Voice Actor And More

Kensho Ono was the voice actor of Floch Foster in Japanese and Matt Shipman in the English dub.

He is of medium height with short and tousled blond hair. Floch’s height was 175 cm when he got introduced to us. Later, he grew to 180 cm from the time skip.
